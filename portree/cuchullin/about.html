Our Story

About Cuchullin

A restaurant named after a legend. A reputation built on shellfish.

The Name

Cú Chulainn

The restaurant takes its name from Cú Chulainn, the great hero of Irish and Scottish mythology. Known as the Hound of Ulster, he was a warrior of impossible strength, fierce loyalty, and legendary appetite.

Born Sétanta on the shores of what is now the Irish Sea, he earned his name after slaying the ferocious guard dog of Culann the smith and offering to take its place until a replacement could be reared. Thus he became Cú Chulainn — the Hound of Culann.

The connection between Ireland and Scotland runs deep through the Gaelic seaways, and the name Cuchullin resonates across the Hebrides. On Skye, the Cuillin mountains bear his name. In Portree, his spirit lives on in a seafood house that values strength of character, warmth of welcome, and a warrior's appetite for a proper feast.

The Restaurant

A Seafood House on Skye

Cuchullin sits in the heart of Portree, overlooking Somerled Square. It is part of the island's seafood dynasty — sister restaurant to Sea Breezes, the beloved harbourside institution that has drawn visitors to Skye for decades.

What we do is simple: we serve the best seafood the cold, clean waters around Skye have to offer, cooked with respect and served without fuss. Our mussels come from Shetland. Our scallops are hand-dived. Our salmon is Scottish. Everything arrives fresh each morning.

There is no pretence here. The dining room is warm and cosy, the welcome is genuine, and the portions are generous. This is food that tastes of where it comes from — the salt, the peat, the rain, the heather. This is Skye on a plate.

How We Work

Walk-in Only

We do not take reservations. Never have, never will.

This is not stubbornness — it is philosophy. A walk-in restaurant is a democratic one. Everyone waits in the same queue. A couple on their first date stands behind a family of five from Glasgow. A solo traveller from Tokyo chats with a hillwalker just off the Cuillin Ridge. The queue is part of the experience. It builds anticipation. It builds community.
