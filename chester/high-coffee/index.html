Our Story

From Kyiv to Chester, with a coffee grinder and a dream

When war came to Ukraine, Vlad and Albina left everything behind. They arrived in Chester under the Homes for Ukraine scheme, carrying little more than their love for Ukrainian coffee culture and a determination to build something new.

In late 2023, they opened High Coffee Bar in the cobbled quiet of Rufus Court — a tucked-away courtyard just inside Chester's ancient city walls. Yellow and blue décor. Ukrainian music on the speakers. Hand-brewed speciality coffee served with genuine warmth.

A small island of Ukraine in a British city. And you are always welcome.

Community

Coffee that gives back

High Coffee Bar is more than a café. It is a community. Vlad and Albina know what it means to arrive in a new country with nothing — and they pay that kindness forward every day.

Through their "buy a coffee for someone homeless" scheme, customers can purchase a drink for someone sleeping rough in Chester. A small act of warmth, no questions asked.

Buy a coffee for someone homeless

Ask at the counter to add a suspended coffee to the board. Someone who needs it can claim it, no questions asked.
