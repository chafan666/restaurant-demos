1990

The Year It All Began

Three Decades on Market Square

The Old Keswickian has been a fixture on Keswick's ancient Market Square for over 30 years, run by the same extended family since the day we first unlocked the door. The name 'Keswickian' means 'a person from Keswick' — and we wear it with pride.

From the beginning, our approach has been simple: take the finest ingredients and treat them with respect. Our wild cod comes from the cold, clean waters of the North Atlantic. Our potatoes are top-grade Lincolnshire Maris Pipers — the kind that turn properly golden and floury when fried. Everything is cooked in 100% non-hydrogenated vegetable oil. No additives, no colours, no preservatives — just good food, honestly made.

The building itself tells a story. Upstairs, beneath old oak beams, our restaurant looks out over Market Square — the same view generations of Keswickians have enjoyed. Downstairs, the takeaway counter hums with the rhythm of a busy chippy: the crackle of fresh batter hitting hot oil, the warm scent of salt and vinegar, the friendly chatter of customers who've been coming here for decades.

Today, we're proud to serve across generations — from grandparents who remember our first day to grandchildren discovering proper fish and chips for the first time. The faces behind the counter may have changed, but the values haven't: honest ingredients, proper cooking, and a warm welcome for everyone who walks through the door.
