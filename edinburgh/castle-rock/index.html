A Proper Cuppa

Duck in from the rain

Castle Rock Cafe sits tucked into the shoulder of Edinburgh's Old Town, a few steps from the clamour of the Royal Mile and a world away from it. The windows steam up on cold afternoons. The radio murmurs. Somewhere a milk jug clinks.

It is the kind of place that does not try too hard, because it does not have to. The coffee is strong, the rolls are generous, and the Millionaire's Shortbread has converted more than one unsuspecting tourist into a regular.

You come in damp from the drizzle and you leave warmer. That is the whole proposition.
