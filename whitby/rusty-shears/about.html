A Slice of Whitby History

The Parlour That Time Forgot

Rusty Shears sits at 4 Silver Street, a narrow side street just off the bustle of Whitby's main thoroughfare. From the outside, you might walk past it. But step through the door, and you enter a world of mismatched china, curiosity cabinets, and the warm glow of a parlour that time forgot.

The name? It comes from the rusty old shears found buried in the courtyard during renovation — now hanging proudly on the wall, a nod to the building's past lives and the layers of history that make Whitby what it is.

By day, sunlight filters through old windows onto tables set with china teapots and vintage saucers. The smell of fresh cake drifts from the kitchen. Regulars chat over full English breakfasts while first-timers gaze around at the curiosity cabinets, trying to take it all in.

The Heart of Rusty Shears

The Courtyard & The Fig Tree

Tucked behind the tea rooms, sheltered from the North Sea breeze, is our courtyard garden. And in the centre of it all, an enormous fig tree — old, gnarled, and utterly magnificent. In summer, its broad leaves create a dappled canopy. In autumn, if you are lucky, you might arrive when the figs are ripe.

It is the perfect spot for a pot of tea on a sunny afternoon, or a gin and tonic as the evening cools. There is nowhere quite like it in Whitby.

Gins. Yes, Really.

When the sun sets on weekends, Rusty Shears transforms. Candles flicker on every table. The china cups make way for copa glasses. And from behind the bar, our collection of over one hundred and fifty gins — from classic London Dry to small-batch Yorkshire craft spirits — comes to life.

It is, quite possibly, the most charming place in Whitby to drink gin.

Four-Legged Friends

Dogs Welcome

We are dog-friendly through and through. Well-behaved dogs are welcome inside the tea rooms and in the courtyard. In fact, every dog that visits gets a complimentary sausage — it has become something of a tradition, and the local dogs know it.

Whitby is a town for walkers, and we think a good walk deserves a good tea shop at the end of it — for both ends of the lead.

A New Chapter

New Management 2024

In 2024, Rusty Shears welcomed new management — a fresh team with a deep love for what this quirky, beloved little spot has always been. The mismatched china stays. The fig tree stays. The unhurried, slightly eccentric charm stays.

What is new? A reinvigorated menu, an even more ambitious gin selection, weekend bistro evenings, and a determination to make Rusty Shears the most memorable stop on any visit to Whitby.

"We are not fast food. We are a relaxed, unhurried experience — and we would not have it any other way."
